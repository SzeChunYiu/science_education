# 15. Social Media Operations Playbook

This document explains how the project should manage social media outputs after scripts exist. It is meant for future sessions that need to publish, review, improve, and standardize multi-platform behavior.

This is an operations playbook, not a strategy essay.

## Role Of Each Platform

Treat platforms differently.

YouTube long-form:

- primary trust and authority layer
- best place for depth
- strongest library value over time

YouTube Shorts:

- discovery layer
- one concept per video
- should feed interest into the long-form archive

X:

- lightweight idea testing
- authority through concise insight
- useful for episode hooks, contrarian observations, and thread-style sequencing

LinkedIn:

- professional credibility
- works well for thoughtful science framing, learning insights, and founder/operator updates

TikTok:

- broad discovery
- strongest when the idea becomes immediately visual or surprising

Do not force every piece of content onto every platform.

---

## Canonical Social Deliverables

For each episode or topic cluster, aim to prepare:

- one long-form YouTube asset
- one YouTube Short
- one X post or thread seed
- one LinkedIn-friendly post when the idea can support a thoughtful professional framing

Optional:

- TikTok-specific edit
- carousel or static infographic
- newsletter/article

---

## Platform Packaging Rules

### YouTube Long-Form

Required:

- title with a specific conceptual promise
- strong thumbnail
- description with references or source notes where appropriate
- pinned comment idea

Avoid:

- vague lecture titles
- thumbnails with too much text
- titles that sound like textbook chapter headings

### YouTube Shorts

Required:

- vertical framing
- immediate hook
- one idea only
- fast payoff

Target shape:

- roughly `35-60s`
- roughly `90-140` spoken words

Avoid:

- long preambles
- multiple concepts in one Short
- slow academic openings

### X

Required:

- one sharp idea
- clean, short phrasing
- platform-native length

Rule:

- default to `<=280` characters unless intentionally creating a thread outside the current simple-post format

Avoid:

- dumping the YouTube description into X
- generic hashtags
- long abstract phrasing

### LinkedIn

Required:

- thoughtful framing
- relevance to learning, discovery, problem-solving, or scientific thinking
- stronger prose than X

Works well:

- “what this concept teaches us”
- “why this historical breakthrough mattered”
- “what most people misunderstand about X”

Avoid:

- meme-heavy tone
- fake inspiration language
- empty corporate phrasing

---

## Social Review Checklist

Before posting, check:

- Is this platform-native?
- Is the opening line strong enough?
- Is the scientific claim still accurate after compression?
- Is the post trying to explain too much?
- Does the CTA fit the platform?
- Is the post too generic to stand out?

If a social post loses scientific accuracy when compressed, do not publish that version. Rewrite it.

---

## Science Accuracy Rule For Social

Compression is allowed. Distortion is not.

Every social asset should preserve:

- causal direction
- scope conditions
- whether a statement is exact, approximate, or analogy
- whether the claim is historical, experimental, or theoretical

Social content may simplify.
It must not imply false certainty or invent causal claims.

## Operating Workflow Per Release

For each publishable episode:

1. choose the lead platform
2. derive the supporting platform variants
3. review platform-native packaging
4. post or queue uploads
5. record links and publish times
6. review metrics later

Default lead-platform logic:

- conceptually deep topic -> long-form YouTube first
- visually surprising idea -> Short first, long-form support after
- fast commentary or topical observation -> X or LinkedIn first

---

## Posting Cadence Guidance

Do not optimize for raw volume alone.

Better rule:

- keep a sustainable cadence
- maintain quality control
- preserve topic continuity

Suggested baseline:

- long-form YouTube on a slower, higher-quality cadence
- Shorts and X more frequently
- LinkedIn selectively, only when the framing genuinely fits

If the team cannot maintain accuracy and packaging quality at a given pace, lower the pace.

---

## Metrics To Watch

YouTube long-form:

- click-through rate
- average view duration
- retention drops
- comments quality
- subscribers gained

YouTube Shorts:

- viewed vs swiped
- retention/completion
- shares
- subscriber conversion

X:

- impressions
- engagement rate
- replies that indicate real curiosity
- clicks to deeper content

LinkedIn:

- impressions
- comments
- reposts
- profile follows
- quality of discussion

Do not optimize purely for vanity metrics. The real target is reach that compounds into trusted audience.

## Editorial Guardrails

Future sessions should follow these platform guardrails:

- do not publish scientifically weak hooks just because they are catchy
- do not use sensational wording that the video cannot satisfy
- do not post content that sounds machine-generated and generic
- do not recycle one exact caption across all platforms
- do not treat Shorts as the only monetization strategy

Preferred behavior:

- clear hook
- accurate payoff
- topic continuity
- useful description/context
- consistent branding without repetition fatigue

---

## Community Management

When comments or replies are handled, aim for:

- correction over defensiveness
- concise answers
- reference to the full video or deeper material where useful
- visible respect for scientific nuance

If a viewer points out a legitimate mistake:

1. verify it
2. acknowledge it clearly
3. correct the content or metadata where practical
4. update the relevant script or workflow note so the mistake is not repeated
