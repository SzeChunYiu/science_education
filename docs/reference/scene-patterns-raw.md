## Transition Patterns Observed

From full script reads, the following structural patterns repeat across nearly all episodes:

1. **Hook → Historical character scene** (0:00–0:30): Opens with dramatic moment or puzzle, often places a historical scientist in their environment
2. **Problem statement → Physics diagram**: Once the historical puzzle is established, a physics diagram appears to make the abstract concrete
3. **Equation introduction → Symbol labeling**: Every equation introduction is followed immediately by labeling each symbol with units and meaning
4. **Derivation step-by-step → Boxed result**: Multi-step algebra is shown line by line; final result gets boxed or highlighted
5. **Abstract concept → Concrete example**: After derivation, worked numbers are substituted (e.g., specific masses, specific distances)
6. **Worked example → Real-world application**: Numbers are then applied to a real scenario (rocket, satellite, ice skater)
7. **Where it works → Where it breaks**: Nearly every episode ends with explicit limits/breakdown conditions
8. **Connection forward → Preview**: Closing beat bridges to the next episode

**Simultaneous elements typically on screen:**
- Most scenes: 1–2 elements (equation + label, or diagram + equation)
- Comparison scenes: 2 columns (side-by-side)
- Table/chart scenes: Table fills center, narrator voice-over
- Portrait scenes: Character portrait (center/left) + name/date lower-third
- Derivation scenes: Sequential lines of algebra, building down the screen
