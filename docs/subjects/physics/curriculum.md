# Physics Curriculum — Full Topic Outline

> "Tell the idea like a story. Teach the subject like a postgraduate course."

Each topic becomes a series of 5-10 short episodes following the historical-dramatic arc.
Episodes are scripted for: YouTube Long, YouTube Short, TikTok, X.

---

## Module 1: Classical Mechanics

### 01. Newton's Laws of Motion ✅ (episode plan done, ep01 scripted)
- 7 episodes: Aristotle's trap → Galileo's ramp → First Law / inertia → Second Law (momentum form) → Third Law → Where Newton breaks → Noether's theorem
- **Key figures:** Aristotle, Galileo, Descartes, Newton, Euler, Noether

### 02. Conservation Laws
- ~6 episodes: What is conserved and why → Momentum conservation → Energy conservation → Work-energy theorem → Angular momentum → Symmetry and conservation (Noether connection)
- **Key figures:** Leibniz, d'Alembert, Lagrange, Noether

### 03. Gravity and Orbits
- ~7 episodes: Ancient models (Ptolemy, Copernicus) → Kepler's laws → Newton's law of gravitation → Inverse square law → Orbital mechanics → Tides and perturbations → Limits of Newtonian gravity
- **Key figures:** Ptolemy, Copernicus, Brahe, Kepler, Newton, Cavendish

### 04. Oscillations and Waves
- ~6 episodes: Simple harmonic motion → Pendulums and springs → Damped and driven oscillations → Resonance → Wave equation → Superposition and interference
- **Key figures:** Hooke, Huygens, Euler, Fourier

### 05. Lagrangian Mechanics
- ~7 episodes: Why F=ma isn't enough → Constraints and generalized coordinates → Principle of least action → Euler-Lagrange equation → Lagrangian for simple systems → Noether's theorem (full) → Hamilton's principle
- **Key figures:** Fermat, Maupertuis, Euler, Lagrange, Hamilton

### 06. Hamiltonian Mechanics
- ~5 episodes: From Lagrangian to Hamiltonian → Phase space → Hamilton's equations → Poisson brackets → Bridge to quantum mechanics
- **Key figures:** Hamilton, Jacobi, Poisson, Liouville

### 07. Rigid Body Dynamics
- ~5 episodes: Rotation and angular velocity → Moment of inertia → Euler's equations → Gyroscopes and precession → The spinning top problem
- **Key figures:** Euler, Poinsot, Foucault

### 08. Central Force Problems
- ~4 episodes: Two-body problem → Effective potential → Kepler orbits revisited → Scattering theory intro
- **Key figures:** Newton, Kepler, Rutherford

## Module 2: Electromagnetism

### 09. Electrostatics
- ~6 episodes: Ancient static electricity → Coulomb's law → Electric field concept → Gauss's law → Electric potential → Conductors and capacitors
- **Key figures:** Thales, Franklin, Coulomb, Gauss, Faraday

### 10. Magnetostatics
- ~5 episodes: Lodestones to compasses → Oersted's discovery → Biot-Savart law → Ampère's law → Magnetic vector potential
- **Key figures:** Gilbert, Oersted, Biot, Savart, Ampère

### 11. Electrodynamics and Maxwell's Equations
- ~8 episodes: Faraday's induction → Faraday's law → Displacement current → Maxwell's equations unified → Electromagnetic waves → Speed of light → Hertz's experiment → Maxwell to modern physics
- **Key figures:** Faraday, Maxwell, Hertz, Heaviside

### 12. Electromagnetic Waves and Optics
- ~5 episodes: Wave solutions of Maxwell → Polarization → Reflection/refraction from EM theory → Diffraction → Interference and coherence
- **Key figures:** Huygens, Young, Fresnel, Maxwell

### 13. Special Relativity (from EM)
- ~7 episodes: The ether problem → Michelson-Morley → Lorentz transformations → Einstein's postulates → Time dilation → Length contraction → E=mc²
- **Key figures:** Lorentz, FitzGerald, Michelson, Morley, Einstein, Minkowski

---

## Module 3: Thermodynamics and Statistical Mechanics

### 14. Classical Thermodynamics
- ~7 episodes: Heat vs temperature → Zeroth law → First law → Heat engines and Carnot → Second law and entropy → Free energy → Third law
- **Key figures:** Carnot, Clausius, Kelvin, Joule, Nernst

### 15. Kinetic Theory
- ~5 episodes: Atoms as billiard balls → Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution → Pressure from collisions → Mean free path → Transport phenomena
- **Key figures:** Bernoulli, Maxwell, Boltzmann

### 16. Statistical Mechanics — Classical
- ~7 episodes: Why count microstates → Boltzmann entropy S=k ln W → Microcanonical ensemble → Canonical ensemble → Partition function → Free energy from Z → Grand canonical ensemble
- **Key figures:** Boltzmann, Gibbs, Einstein

### 17. Statistical Mechanics — Quantum
- ~6 episodes: Identical particles problem → Bose-Einstein statistics → Fermi-Dirac statistics → Blackbody radiation → Bose-Einstein condensation → Fermi gas and metals
- **Key figures:** Planck, Bose, Einstein, Fermi, Dirac

### 18. Phase Transitions
- ~5 episodes: Order and disorder → Ising model → Mean field theory → Critical exponents → Universality and renormalization group (preview)
- **Key figures:** van der Waals, Ising, Onsager, Wilson, Kadanoff

---

## Module 4: Quantum Mechanics

### 19. Origins of Quantum Theory
- ~7 episodes: Blackbody catastrophe → Planck's quantum → Photoelectric effect → Bohr's atom → de Broglie waves → Compton scattering → Old quantum theory fails
- **Key figures:** Planck, Einstein, Bohr, de Broglie, Compton

### 20. Wave Mechanics
- ~6 episodes: Schrödinger's equation → Probability interpretation → Particle in a box → Harmonic oscillator → Hydrogen atom → Tunneling
- **Key figures:** Schrödinger, Born, Heisenberg

### 21. Matrix Mechanics and Formalism
- ~6 episodes: Heisenberg's matrices → Dirac notation → Observables and operators → Commutators → Uncertainty principle → Measurement problem
- **Key figures:** Heisenberg, Born, Jordan, Dirac, von Neumann

### 22. Angular Momentum and Spin
- ~6 episodes: Orbital angular momentum → Spherical harmonics → Stern-Gerlach experiment → Spin → Addition of angular momenta → Clebsch-Gordan
- **Key figures:** Stern, Gerlach, Pauli, Dirac

### 23. Identical Particles and Atoms
- ~5 episodes: Indistinguishability → Pauli exclusion principle → Helium atom → Periodic table → Slater determinants
- **Key figures:** Pauli, Slater, Hartree, Fock

### 24. Perturbation Theory and Approximations
- ~5 episodes: Why exact solutions run out → Time-independent perturbation theory → Degenerate perturbation theory → Variational method → WKB approximation
- **Key figures:** Rayleigh, Schrödinger, Brillouin, Kramers, Wentzel

### 25. Quantum Entanglement and Foundations
- ~6 episodes: EPR paradox → Bell's theorem → Bell test experiments → Decoherence → Interpretations → Quantum information preview
- **Key figures:** Einstein, Bohr, Bell, Aspect, Zeilinger

---

## Module 5: Relativity

### 26. Special Relativity (deep)
- ~6 episodes: Spacetime geometry → Four-vectors → Relativistic mechanics → Relativistic energy-momentum → Electrodynamics in SR → Paradoxes resolved
- **Key figures:** Einstein, Minkowski, Lorentz

### 27. General Relativity
- ~8 episodes: Equivalence principle → Curved spacetime → Metric tensor → Geodesics → Einstein field equations → Schwarzschild solution → Black holes → Gravitational waves
- **Key figures:** Einstein, Schwarzschild, Hilbert, Wheeler, LIGO team

### 28. Cosmology
- ~6 episodes: Expanding universe → Friedmann equations → Big Bang → Cosmic microwave background → Dark matter evidence → Dark energy and acceleration
- **Key figures:** Friedmann, Lemaître, Hubble, Penzias, Wilson, Riess, Perlmutter

## Module 6: Mathematical Methods (interwoven)

### 29. Vector Calculus for Physics
- ~4 episodes: Gradient, divergence, curl → Line and surface integrals → Stokes and Gauss theorems → Applications in E&M

### 30. Differential Equations in Physics
- ~4 episodes: ODEs in mechanics → PDEs: wave, heat, Laplace → Separation of variables → Green's functions

### 31. Complex Analysis for Physics
- ~4 episodes: Analytic functions → Contour integration → Residue theorem → Applications (scattering, signal processing)

### 32. Group Theory for Physics
- ~5 episodes: Symmetry and groups → Representations → Lie groups and algebras → SU(2) and angular momentum → Gauge symmetry preview
- **Key figures:** Galois, Lie, Noether, Weyl, Wigner

## Module 7: Quantum Field Theory

### 33. Why QFT?
- ~5 episodes: Particles are not enough → Relativity + QM = trouble → Klein-Gordon equation → Dirac equation → Antimatter prediction
- **Key figures:** Klein, Gordon, Dirac, Anderson

### 34. Classical Field Theory
- ~4 episodes: Fields as dynamical systems → Lagrangian density → Noether's theorem for fields → Symmetries and conserved currents
- **Key figures:** Lagrange, Hamilton, Noether

### 35. Canonical Quantization
- ~6 episodes: From particles to fields → Quantizing the scalar field → Creation and annihilation operators → Fock space → The vacuum isn't empty → Normal ordering
- **Key figures:** Dirac, Fock, Jordan

### 36. Quantum Electrodynamics (QED)
- ~7 episodes: Quantizing the electromagnetic field → Photons → Electron-photon interaction → Feynman diagrams → Tree-level processes → Loop corrections → Renormalization
- **Key figures:** Feynman, Schwinger, Tomonaga, Dyson

### 37. Path Integrals
- ~5 episodes: Sum over histories → Feynman's path integral → Propagators → Generating functionals → Connection to statistical mechanics
- **Key figures:** Feynman, Dirac

### 38. Renormalization Group
- ~6 episodes: Infinities in QFT → Regularization → Renormalization → Running coupling constants → Wilson's RG → Universality and effective field theory
- **Key figures:** Bethe, Tomonaga, Schwinger, Feynman, Wilson, Kadanoff

### 39. Non-Abelian Gauge Theory
- ~6 episodes: From QED to Yang-Mills → SU(2) and SU(3) → Gauge invariance → Gluons and color → Asymptotic freedom → Confinement
- **Key figures:** Yang, Mills, Gell-Mann, Gross, Wilczek, Politzer

### 40. The Standard Model
- ~7 episodes: Electroweak unification → Higgs mechanism → Spontaneous symmetry breaking → W and Z bosons → The full Standard Model Lagrangian → Predictions and tests → What's missing
- **Key figures:** Glashow, Weinberg, Salam, Higgs, 't Hooft, Veltman

### 41. Beyond the Standard Model (preview)
- ~4 episodes: Neutrino masses → Hierarchy problem → Supersymmetry idea → Grand unification and quantum gravity outlook
- **Key figures:** Pontecorvo, Wess, Zumino, Georgi, Glashow

## Production Order (recommended)

Start with topics that have the richest historical stories and most visual potential:

**Wave 1 — Foundation & Hook Content:**
1. Newton's Laws (in progress)
2. Gravity and Orbits (Kepler, Newton — great visual story)
3. Origins of Quantum Theory (Planck, Einstein — dramatic revolution)
4. Electrodynamics and Maxwell (unification story)

**Wave 2 — Building the Narrative:**
5. Special Relativity
6. Classical Thermodynamics (Carnot, entropy)
7. Conservation Laws
8. Wave Mechanics (Schrödinger)

**Wave 3 — Deepening:**
9. Statistical Mechanics — Classical
10. General Relativity
11. Angular Momentum and Spin
12. Lagrangian Mechanics

**Wave 4 — Advanced & QFT:**
13-41. Remaining topics building toward QFT climax

This order maximizes: audience hook potential, prerequisite flow, visual diversity, and historical drama.
